In 1980s, many buildings in East Harlem were allocated to the government to relieve the issue of housing shortage. Till now there are still large proportion of city-owned buildings there. The turnaround of Harlem started in 1990s. It is transferring into a new residential neighborhoods with distinct feature and substantial potential. With a great number of new buildings coming, East Harlem becomes attractive places for residents, private developments and cultural centers. Those factors make it the district with the largest rise of house value.

This diagram is intended to illustrate the land use condition and the ownership of the properties in Harlem. To be specific, I want display the similarities and differences among these three neighborhoods to help potential developers make right decision.


To display the similarity and diversity in land use composition among these three neighborhoods, multi-ring pie diagram was chosen. The diagram is not complicated. Each ring stands for the composition of main land uses in East Harlem, West Harlem and Central Harlem. Multi-family uses are given similar blue color, mix-use land is orange distinguished from the blue ones. One & two family building accounting to small proportion thus is showed in gray to weaken the importance.
